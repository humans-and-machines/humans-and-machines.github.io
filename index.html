Humans and Machines Lab

Welcome! The Princeton Humans and Machines Lab aims to understand and steer the behavior of machine learning systems—especially large language models (LLMs)—within social and informational environments. We use causal inference, experimental audits, and computational methods to analyze how these systems impact human behavior and society. By treating LLMs both as subjects of study and as tools for modeling human cognition, we generate insights that inform the design of algorithms, platforms, and policies for socially beneficial AI.

Code for our projects is available at https://github.com/humans-and-machines.
